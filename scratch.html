Ballet is Harder Than People Think

Resume Connect 4


But how exactly?

  • Physical Strength
    1. Ballerinas can carry 3 times their body weight on their big toes.
    2. Dancers on professional companies spend 6-9 hours per day dancing, 5-6 days a week on average.
    3. Many professional football players take ballet classes to improve their balance, strength, agility, and flexibility.
  • Mental Health
    1. Ballet is very competitive -- only about 2-3% of dancers make it to professional status.
    2. Ballet puts a large emphasis on perfection, often leading to dancers pushing themselves past their limits and still not feeling like enough.
    3. With the pressure of being in just a leotard and tights, constantly facing mirrors, and sometimes remarks from other students and teachers, many dancers face severe body image issues.
  • Focus and Resilience
    1. Dancers must have the ability to quickly learn and remember choreography.
    2. In addition to remembering choreography, dancers must be able to execute the choreography with the correct technique and artistry.
    3. Ballerinas must also dedicate time outside of time to take care of their bodies and prevent injuries.
